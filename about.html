Bishwa Zakir Manzil (BZM), as the name suggests, is a global destination for the devotees and believers who want to visit the place to achieve a level of spiritual experience through Zikir.

The Arabic term Zikir, literally means: “Remembrance” or “Recollection”. It is widely believed that through these chanting in silence or in rhythmic repetitive utterances a devotee attains the level of perfection. BZM has a turnover of more than 10 million visitors yearly. The establishment is embodied with the mystical teachings of Islam. And devotees visit to learn and nurture themselves with knowledge in the form of inner enlightenment.

In the early 20th century, Bangladesh saw the rise of one of the most influential and instrumental Sufi Saint; Bishwa Wali Khwaja Faridpuri (QSA) a philosopher, a visionary, and a humanitarian.

Considered one of the most historic and iconic Sufi Saint’s in Bangladesh; Bishwa Wali Khwaja Faridpuri (QSA) established the teachings of the Naqshbandi Mujjadidia school of thought (link to wikipedia- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naqshbandi). Hailing from a noble zamindar family, Bishwa Wali Khwaja Faridpuri (QSA) was summoned to devote his life to Khwaja Enayetpuri’s Darbar where he cultivated the teachings of the Naqshbandi Mujaddidi tariqah. He went on to establish BZM during the 1940’s in Atroshi, Faridpur. The institute was created for his millions of devotees who are welcomed from all parts of Bangladesh and internationally. The institute, respectfully known as Bishwa Zaker Manzil (BZM) is home to millions of Sufi followers from all religions and backgrounds. Yearly events take place at BZM that are conducted through prayers, recitations, meditations, and speeches.
